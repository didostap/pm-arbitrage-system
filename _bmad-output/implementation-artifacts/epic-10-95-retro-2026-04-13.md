## Challenges & Growth Areas

1. **180% scope growth — correction model failure.** Planned 5, shipped 14. Not a planning failure — a scope classification failure. Three distinct scopes (infrastructure, adjacent QoL, backtest quality) wore one epic number. Correction model works for defined scope; doesn't cover when implementation reveals entirely new work categories.

2. **Deferred debt ledger grew net +7.** Entered with 6 items (from 10.9 retro), exit with ~13. Accumulation rate unsustainable. Individual deferrals defensible in isolation; system-level pattern is not.

3. **Agreement #26 items slipped two full epics.** configService type safety and route prefix validation (committed in 10.9 retro, planned for early 10.95) never surfaced when course corrections consumed capacity. Preventive work loses to curative work unless mechanically enforced.

4. **BacktestEngineService re-grew from 592 to ~764 lines.** ~17 lines per story of accretion across stories 8-14. Facade gravity well — every filter, guard, and counter gravitates to where the loop lives. Trajectory would hit 900+ again by end of 10.96 without intervention.

5. **5 Prisma migrations in 12 days.** Zero migration issues (credit to execution and test coverage), but pace risk. Additive changes should batch; structural changes should isolate.

6. **Previous retro follow-through: 3/6 completed, 2/6 not addressed.** Below the 100% standard established in Epics 10.8 and 10.9. Root cause identified (preventive vs curative priority inversion). Addressed by Agreement #34.

## Key Insights & Learnings

1. **The correction model works for defined scope; it doesn't cover discovery of entirely new work categories.** 180% growth is a scope classification failure. With the split rule (Agreement #31), original 10.95 would show ~40% growth (within model), and the quality pipeline would be a separate zero-planned discovery sprint. Both numbers honest.

2. **Small, single-concern stories produce better code, faster review, and cleaner execution.** Stories 8-14 were the cleanest of the epic. Each was one filter or guard, TDD'd, reviewed, done in 2-3 hours. The lesson is not "skip review for small stories" — it's "write smaller stories." Inform 10.96 story design.

3. **Facades are gravity wells.** Every feature gravitates to where the loop lives unless the story AC specifies otherwise. Fix: build decomposition into feature stories (Agreement #27 extended). Winston designs service boundaries in each 10.96 story AC. Prevention over remediation.

4. **Preventive work loses to curative work unless mechanically enforced.** Structural guards, agreement items, debt resolution — all lose priority when bugs and features compete for attention. Fix: agreement enforcement at sprint planning slot zero (Agreement #34).

5. **Cascading discovery needs investigation-first *after the first fix lands*, not before capability exists to diagnose.** The diagnostic sweep gate (Agreement #32) applies after the first fix in a quality category reveals a pattern, not before the tooling can surface the issues.

6. **Proof-of-concept-first approach validated.** Story 1 establishes pattern, subsequent stories apply template. Carry into 10.96: first porting story establishes backtest-to-live pattern, stories 2-3 follow template.

7. **Process that doesn't prove itself gets removed.** Per Arbi's directive: if any of the four new agreements aren't earning their keep by the 10.96 retro, they get cut. Agreements exist to protect execution, not replace it.

## Final Assessment

Epic 10.95 delivered 14 stories in 12 days: a 337 GB → sub-50 GB storage migration with 90%+ compression, a backtest engine decomposition (917 → 592 lines), a 30x performance improvement, and seven backtest quality fixes that define Epic 10.96's scope. Zero incidents, 3,793 tests passing, clean execution throughout.

The 180% scope growth is the headline. It's not a planning failure — it's a scope classification failure. Three distinct scopes (infrastructure, adjacent QoL, backtest quality pipeline) wore one epic number. Agreement #31 (split rule) ensures clean correction model signal going forward. The backtest quality cascade (stories 8-14) was discovery-driven — each fix revealed the next layer. Agreement #32 (diagnostic sweep gate) addresses this: sweep after the first quality-category fix, before writing more.

The deferred debt ledger grew from 6 to 13 items — net +7. Individual deferrals are defensible; the system-level trajectory is not. Agreement #33 (debt budget) forces net-zero-or-negative resolution per epic. The two-epic deferral of Agreement #26 structural guards is the sharpest failure. Agreement #34 (slot-zero enforcement) makes this mechanical rather than discretionary.

The live trading engine has been disabled since Epic 10.7's profitability gap discovery. Everything since — backtesting (10.9), storage optimization and quality fixes (10.95), and now alignment (10.96) — builds toward re-enablement with confidence. Epic 10.96 is the last gate: 4 stories, tight scope, known implementation sites, 5 fixes to port. The team enters 10.96 with the scope already defined, the patterns already proven, and the preparation plan locked.
