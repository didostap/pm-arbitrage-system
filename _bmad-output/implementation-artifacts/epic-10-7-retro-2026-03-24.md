## Challenges & Growth Areas

1. **Code review noise: 19% actionable rate.** 245 raw findings across 9 stories, only 47 actionable (19 patches + 24 deferred fixes + 4 bad-spec corrections). 155 rejected as pre-existing, by-design, or incorrect pattern-matching. Root cause: Lad MCP scopes to files, not diffs — reviewers flag everything wrong with a 1,400-line file, not just the 30 lines this story added.

2. **Soft discipline follow-through failed again.** Agreements #27 (design sketch) and #29 (naming walkthrough) had zero evidence of execution across 9 stories. Third consecutive retro confirming the pattern: honor-system steps without artifacts don't happen.

3. **God Objects grew during feature work.** ExitMonitorService went from ~1,438 to ~1,547 lines (+109) with chunking logic, shadow fixes, and PnL persistence. ExecutionService grew +35 lines (dual-leg gate). Feature work inevitably grows the hottest files — refactoring must follow, not interleave.

4. **ConfigAccessor circular DI.** 10-7-6 used ConfigService instead of ConfigAccessor to avoid circular dependency injection. The rewiring debt from 10-5-2 remains unresolved for 7+ services. Module boundary reshuffling in 10.8 may provide natural resolution.

5. **Secondary Lad MCP reviewer unreliable.** Failed with provider errors on 10-7-1 (OpenRouter 400), partially unreliable on 10-7-8 and 10-7-9. kimi-k2.5 served as functional fallback but wasn't pre-planned.

## Key Insights & Lessons Learned

1. **Evidence-based story creation eliminates scope creep.** When every story cites specific data (position count, failure rate, edge decay magnitude), there's no room for "while we're in there" scope growth. Nine stories, zero course corrections within epic.

2. **Code review signal is real but needs context scoping.** The 47 actionable findings included TOCTOU bugs (10-7-9), God File extraction (10-7-8: 725→547 lines), repository abstractions (10-7-4), and Decimal precision fixes (10-7-3). Fix the noise with better context priming, not fewer review layers.

3. **"If it's in the story file, it happens."** The definitive pattern across three retros. VWAP knowledge transfer (in story context) worked. Design sketch (separate activity) didn't. Soft disciplines without artifacts are aspirational — Agreement #30 closes this permanently.

4. **Investigation-first + code review follow-up = disproportionate value.** 10-7-4's investigation found a trivial bug, but code review D1-D6 fixes created `closePosition()`, `updateStatusWithAccumulatedPnl()`, `RiskStateDivergenceEvent`, and `sumClosedPnlByDateRange` — infrastructure that benefits every future story.

5. **Feature work and refactoring are sequential, not parallel.** 10.7 grew ExitMonitorService by 109 lines while knowing it needs decomposition. This is correct — interleaving refactoring with feature delivery creates merge conflicts and split attention. The sequence 10.7 features → 10.8 refactoring is right.
